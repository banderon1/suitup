FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

How Can Youth prepare for this youth conference?

  • If possible obtain a "Preach My Gospel" (large or small manual is fine) book to bring with you to the conference.
  • Review and pack only items on the packing list, please don't over pack or bring extra items.

Who can attend youth conference 2016?

Any youth 14-18 years of age. Also included are 13 year olds who will be entering high school in the 2016-2017 school year, as well as any 19 year old who have just graduated from high school in 2016.

How many chaperons does each ward need to provide?

2 chaperones from each ward (preferably a male and a female). Housing is available for chaperons, please make note of this on the registration form. Every chaperon needs to register.

Will youth be housed with friends?

The registration form asks for a buddy request for housing, we will do our best to honor those requests.

Where will the youth be housed?

In homes of members of the Ventura Stake.

How much does youth conference cost?

$50 per participant (youth or chaperon)- covers meals, snacks, activities, supplies for service project, DJ for dances, any other equipment rental.

Who do I contact if my youth has any special needs/considerations to accommodate prior to the event?

Please contact our registration coordinator, Donna Smith with any concerns.

When is registration due?

By Sunday May 15th, 2016.

What time is check on Thursday the 14th?

4-4:30 pm, at the Ventura Stake Center

What time is pick up at the end of the conference, Saturday July 16th?

2pm, at the Ventura Stake Center

Where will the Conference take place and what will the youth be doing during the 3 days?

See schedule above.

Speakers

Get familiar with our wonderful leaders.

Kelend Mills

Katherine Mills

Brother and Sister Mills are members of the 19th Ward in the Mapleton Utah Stake. Brother Mills currently serves as a member of the stake high council.

Before moving to Mapleton Brother and Sister Mills lived in Spanish Fork where Brother Mills served as a counselor in the Spanish Fork Utah Canyon View Stake presidency, as a bishop, and as a counselor in a bishopric. Brother Mills served as a full-time missionary in the Japan Nagoya Mission from 1985 to 1987. He and Sister Mills and their family served together in Japan as Brother Mills served as president of the Japan Fukuoka Mission from 2003 to 2006.

Brother Mills works for the Missionary Department. He is currently the Director of International MTCs.

Brother Mills was born in Afton, Wyoming to Richard and Betty Burton Mills and was raised in Laketown, Utah in the Bear Lake area. Sister Mills grew up in the Salt Lake Valley. They were married in the Salt Lake Temple on June 1, 1989 and have three children.
